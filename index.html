A planet-scale city-builder & nature simulation

Build a civilization on a living Earth — and look at what you were given to tend until you feel the wonder of something greater than yourself.

The Vision

A world meant to be felt, not explained.

Most games tell you what to think. Ours simply builds a world true and beautiful enough that you feel it on your own. We never put words in your mouth or a meter on your screen for wonder. We hold the world up to the light — and let it speak for itself.

What We Build Toward

Two feelings, held together.

The first

Awe

The vastness and the majesty — the feeling of being small before something ordered and beautiful beyond our making. Zoom from a single blade of grass to the curve of the planet, and let the scale do the rest.

The second

Stewardship

“I am steward of something sacred.” The world is entrusted to you, not handed over to be used up. You live in it, not above it.

Held together, they make reverence — the care of someone who knows the garden is not their own.

What Holds It Together

Nothing you do is without meaning.

A single act — of justice or of neglect — ripples from one street to the whole world. Small faithfulness matters at the largest scale, and even the world’s ruin is never the final word: what is broken can be restored. That shape is built into everything the game simulates.

Our promise

We show; we never preach. The world earns your wonder rather than demanding it.

Our measure

No way of life is crowned by its name. A society is weighed only by its fruit — how it treats the weakest among it, and whether it leads people toward liberty.

A Living Earth

The world is not a backdrop. It was here before you.

A whole planet, modeled on the real Earth — its mountains, coasts, climate, and sky. It runs on real forces, not stagecraft, so it behaves the way a world should: indifferent to your plans, generous beyond your labor, alive whether you are watching or not.

Planet Scale

The whole Earth, from orbit to underfoot

Stand on real geography — recognizable coastlines, ranges, and rivers drawn from true elevation data. Rise from street level to the curve of the planet in one continuous sweep. The land can be sculpted and shaped, and it remembers what you do to it.

Weather & Seasons

A true global climate

Wind, pressure, and temperature flow from real atmospheric circulation. Storms form and travel; seasons turn with the tilt of the world; deserts, monsoons, and snow belong to the places they would on Earth. The weather is a living system you can read — and feel.

Water That Flows

Rivers and seas carved by the land itself

Water gathers where real elevation sends it — rivers run downhill, lakes fill basins, coasts meet the sea. It moves, it catches the light, and over time its very color answers to the life within it.

Sky & Light

A real sun, real nights, and the aurora

The sun crosses the sky on its true astronomical path; dawn and dusk arrive where and when they should. Clouds drift with the wind the weather actually drives, and on the right nights, the aurora answers to real space weather overhead.

Living Ecology

Forests, grasslands, and the breath of the wind

Vegetation belongs to its biome — the right trees and grasses for the climate, swaying in the very wind the weather drives. Wildlife moves through it. The living world fills the land you tend, and it flourishes or fades by how you keep it.

The People

Every person is a soul, not a statistic.

The people who fill your world are not interchangeable dots. Each is born, grows, ages, and passes. They carry families, faces, faith, and frailty — and they respond, in the end, to how they are treated.

Born & Mortal

Lives that begin, age, and end

People are born and grow old as the world turns — childhood, adulthood, and age all read on the body and the face. Time is real here, and so are its seasons of life.

Families & Lineage

Households form; bloodlines run deep

People pair, raise families, and pass their traits to their children. You can follow a lineage across generations — faithfulness, fortune, and resemblance carried down the years — so the story of a place is the story of its families.

Faces & Bodies

No two people alike

Every person has a distinct face and body, inherited from their parents and shaped by the whole range of real human variation — height, build, age, and ancestry. The crowd you walk through is genuinely a crowd of individuals.

Culture & Belief

Communities with their own way of life

Regions carry their own faiths, customs, and dress, blending naturally across borders the way real cultures do. What a people believes and values shapes how they live — and how they respond to the world you build around them.

Mind & Wellbeing

People flourish — or they suffer

Wellbeing is real. Crowding, neglect, hardship, and loneliness weigh on people; safety, beauty, community, and care lift them. A city that looks prosperous can still be quietly breaking its people — and you will see it.

Movement

People come, and people go

Populations move toward opportunity and away from hardship. Tend a place well and it draws newcomers; let it decline and it empties. Crowds live their daily lives around you — and they even speak, in a voice all their own.

Build a Civilization

From a single street to a nation, you give the world its shape.

You do not just place buildings. You found communities, name them, govern them, and watch them take on a life of their own — for better or for worse.

Name Everything

Charter a hamlet; raise it to a nation

Draw the borders and give the names — hamlet, village, town, neighborhood, city, county, province, country. Every tier is yours to found and shape, and watching a place you named grow from a few homes into a great city is the heart of the game.

Zone & Shape

Decide how the land is used

Paint zones, subdivide land into lots, and set the character of each district. The world fills in around your intent — homes, work, and services growing where you make room for them.

Govern

Government that is alive

Nations can take any real form — and none is simply “good” or “evil.” Governments shift with their people, rise and fall, can be reformed or overthrown, and are judged by their fruit: whether they do justice, protect the vulnerable, and lead toward liberty. Set policy and taxes, and feel the consequences travel up and down.

Two Ways to Play

Greenfield

The only light

Build the first and only civilization on an untouched world. A canvas for the patient steward — just you, the land, and what you choose to make of it.

Living Nations

A world alive around you

Share the Earth with rival nations that build, trade, scheme, and war on their own. The world is shifting whether you act or not — ally, compete, or stand against the tide.

A World That Responds

Your choices are never inert.

Everything is connected. Tend one thing and another flourishes; harm one and another grieves. The world keeps an honest account — and answers, in time, to how you have kept it.

Ripple Effect

From one street to the whole world

A single decision — a tax, a kindness, a neglect — spreads outward: a household, a neighborhood, a region, a nation. And the largest forces press back down onto the smallest lives. Nothing happens in isolation.

Rest & Renewal

The living land must rest to flourish

Nature does not yield endlessly. Soil tires, fields need fallow, forests renew through their cycles. Push the land without rest and it falters; honor its rhythms and it gives back — a quiet truth woven into how the world works.

Health & Care

Illness spreads — and can be met

Disease moves through crowding, filth, and neglect, and weighs hardest on those without care. Clean streets, good services, and the hospitals you build are the difference between an outbreak contained and a crisis that lingers. Hardship is felt, never glorified.

Decay & Repair

Neglect rots; tending restores

Let a place fall into disrepair and decline takes hold — blight, vermin, and despair feeding on one another. But nothing is beyond redemption: invest, repair, and care, and even a ruined district can be brought back to life.

Justice & Consequence

A society reaps what it sows

How a people treats its weakest is the truest measure of its health — and the world keeps that account. Govern with justice and mercy and a society rises; rule by neglect and greed and it groans, and may fall. Ruin and restoration are both always within reach.
